Why This Exists

Solving the age-old chametz mystery

Every year, the same problem: 10 pieces hidden before Bedikat Chametz — and by the time the search begins, someone can't remember where piece #7 went. Now there's an answer.

😰
The Old Problem
10 pieces hidden throughout the house. No record of where. Search over — still missing one. Did we find it? Was it 9 or 10? The mitzvah ends in mild chaos every year.
✨
The New Solution
Each piece is recorded with a photo, room, hint, and precision GPS snapshot. Every family member sees the live count. The app guides searchers directly to each piece.
Full Feature List

Everything the app does

🏠
Family Room System
One family member creates a room and gets a 5-letter code. Others join instantly by entering the code — no accounts, no passwords, no App Store required.
Multi-user
🔄
Real-Time Live Sync
Powered by Firebase Realtime Database. The moment someone hides or finds a piece, every family member's screen updates instantly — even across different rooms or floors of the house.
Firebase
🫙
10-Piece Tracker
All 10 pieces displayed on a clear list. Each card shows its status, who hid it, when, which room, and any hint — color-coded green, orange, or grey at a glance.
Core
📍
Precision GPS Snapshot
When hiding a piece, the app samples GPS 5 times over ~8 seconds and computes a weighted average — the best possible indoor location fix, shown as ±Xm accuracy on each piece card.
Apple-style
📷
Hiding Spot Photo
Snap a photo of exactly where the piece is hidden using the device camera. Compressed automatically for fast sync. Shown to searchers as the ultimate visual clue when GPS alone isn't precise enough indoors.
Visual Clue
🔥
Proximity Finder
Tap any hidden piece to open the live Proximity Finder. A glowing orb pulses and changes color as you walk closer: 🔥 Right Here · ♨️ Very Close · 🌡 Getting Warm · ❄️ Getting Cold · 🧊 Far Away.
Find My–style
🧭
Compass Direction Arrow
When more than ~4 meters from the piece, a directional arrow rotates toward the target using live GPS bearing combined with the device's orientation sensor for compass-accurate pointing.
Navigation
💬
Text Hints
Add a short custom hint when hiding each piece — "behind the lamp," "under the seat cushion." Shown alongside the photo in the Finder so searchers have every possible clue to work with.
Clue System
🔔
Push Notifications
Opt in to get notified the instant another family member finds a piece — even when the app is in the background or the screen is locked. Works on both iOS (Add to Home Screen) and Android.
Live Alerts
📋
Activity Feed
A timestamped log of every action in the session — who hid what and when, who found what, who joined the room. A running family record of the entire search from start to finish.
History

The full blessing before the search — in Hebrew script, transliteration, and English translation — with a "Hear Pronunciation" button that reads it aloud via the device speaker. Never fumble for the siddur in the dark.

The Aramaic nullification declaration in Hebrew, transliteration, English, and audio. Prompted automatically after all 10 pieces are found. Includes a reminder that it must be understood, not merely recited.

Four-tab guide covering: Quick Start walkthrough, In-Depth halachic background, What You Need (supply list with explanations), and Timing reference for Erev Pesach. Sourced from halachic authorities.
Education

How It Works

Start to finish in 5 steps

1
Create a Room
The family admin opens the app, enters their name, and taps "Create Room." A unique 5-letter code is generated instantly. Share it by text or WhatsApp — family members join by entering the code on their own phones. Everyone is now synced to the same 10 pieces.
2
Hide the 10 Pieces
Before nightfall, anyone in the room uses the Hide tab to log each piece: select the room, add an optional text hint, snap a photo of the exact spot, and tap Save. The app takes a 5-sample GPS average for the most accurate location fix possible. All family members see the piece appear on their screens the moment it's saved.
3
Recite the Brachah
Once night falls and the family gathers, tap ✡️ to open the Brachah screen. Hebrew script, transliteration, and English translation are all displayed together. Tap the audio button to hear it pronounced clearly. Recite together before the search begins.
4
Search with the Proximity Finder
Switch to the Find tab. Tap any hidden piece to open the Proximity Finder: a glowing orb pulses hotter as you get closer, a compass arrow points the way, and the hiding photo plus text hint are always visible. When you've found it — tap "Found It!" and everyone's screen updates live. Push notifications alert family members in the background.
5
Recite Kol Chamira & Burn
When the 10th piece is found, confetti fills the screen and a full completion summary shows who found each piece. Tap "Recite Kol Chamira" to nullify any missed chametz — Hebrew, transliteration, English, and audio all provided. Then bag all 10 pieces with the feather and wooden spoon and burn them the next morning before the deadline.
